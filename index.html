III. Big lie

In his early research, Mintzberg found out what top managers of companies were actually spending their working time on. As noted earlier, business strategy borrowed heavily from elements of military strategy, but Mintzberg found that top managers spent the lion's share of their time not on developing grand plans, moving their 'troops' back and forth, pondering maneuvers and tactics, or thinking about how to win a battle. Mintzberg was convinced that shining generalship is a myth, and the reality is feverish improvisation, as most of the working time of top managers is spent solving pressing issues, resolving unexpected problems, and adjusting to new conditions. They were almost constantly answering phone calls or in urgent meetings, trying to minimize the amount of damage.
